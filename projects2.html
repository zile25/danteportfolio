Project 2: Creating & Remixing 3D Designs

Return to Main Menu

Part 1: CAD Orientation

I really enjoyed using both Fusion and Tinkercad for the first time. My learning process involved watching tutorials, then supplementing them with ChatGPT and additional research. While Tinkercad was more user-friendly, I appreciated the power of Fusion once I learned its advanced tools like Loft and Shell. My rocket models turned out well — Tinkercad gave a more playful look, while Fusion produced a clean, industrial design.

Part 2: Tinkercad Remix

When browsing online 3D models, I found various F1 track models and an old F1 logo stand that inspired me. I removed excess material from the stand and integrated the track design into it using “hole” shapes in Tinkercad. After combining the models, I sliced and printed them with 20% infill. The letters fit snugly into their stands, and the result achieved its goal — an elegant and interesting display piece. In the future, I could merge the stand and track edges more seamlessly for an even cleaner look.

Part 3: Fusion Remix

For my Fusion remix, I designed a custom Xbox controller stand. I incorporated an F1 track and a driver’s logo into the design, using precise alignment and mesh grouping in Fusion. After converting to a solid, I exported to Prusa Slicer with supports enabled. The print turned out strong and functional. If I were to improve it, I’d add a second shelf for another controller and reinforce it with more infill for durability.
