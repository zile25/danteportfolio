Photos Around Campus Representing Diffierent Design Principles

1. Easier Navigation for Those Without Sight

Handrails on stairs are a helpful balancing and navigation aid for many people of differing ability levels, and especially useful for those with limited sight

2. Forms of Assistance in the Library

Tech, research, and borrowing support all avalibile in close proximity within the library

3. Spacious Ramp and Staircase, Great for an Aging Population

Spacious ramp and multi-railing staircase are more accessible to those who have a harder moving around which is a common symptom of aging

4. Candy Shelf

A great selection of candy shows design for the food that kids love the most

5. Outdoor Sprial Ramp

In lieu of stairs, a ramp is provided for those using wheeled mobile gear to enter Gzang

6. Small Door in a Large Hallway

Glass panels that block accesibility shows design with physical barriers that could be removed

7. Lack of Information

A bus stop on the left side of the road when the bus' doors are on the right shows a design where key considerations may have been overlooked

8. Lack of Help

A long highway separates our campus with a crosswalk and a skybridge but the designers left a long stretch of road without a safe way to cross on the western side

9. Ultra Modern Design

Lots of glass, sharp edges, and a focus on sustainablity show a building built with a historic specificity set in the future

10. Design for Sustainablity

A program to reuse take-out containers reduces waste and this collection bin is a key piece of this system

11. Designing for Health and Well-Being

Large windows let natural light in and efficiently power students through a long day of classes

12. Public Bike Repair Tools and Guides

Designing to promote free and easy access to bike repair will get more people riding and help bikes to last more miles on the road
